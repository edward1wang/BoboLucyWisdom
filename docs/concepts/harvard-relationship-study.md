# Harvard Study of Adult Development

The **longest-running longitudinal study of human happiness** — tracking 724 men across their entire lives for **80 years**.

## Study Design

- Started 1938
- Two groups: Harvard students + Boston impoverished youth
- Interviews every 2 years
- Health, career, relationships tracked

## Hypothesis Failed

Researchers expected: wealth, IQ, genetics predict longevity.

## Actual Findings

**The #1 predictor of health and longevity at age 80:**

> **Warm, close relationships at age 50**

- Quality > Quantity
- High-conflict marriage worse than divorce
- Loneliness = silent killer (chronic pain, cognitive decline)

## Key Quote

"The only thing that matters in life is your relationships."

— George Vaillant, study director

## Implications

Most people optimize career/wealth at ages 30-40, neglecting relationships. The regret at 70-80: **not spending time with loved ones**.

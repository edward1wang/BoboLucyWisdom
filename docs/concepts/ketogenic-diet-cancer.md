# Ketogenic Diet and Cancer

The **metabolic theory of cancer**: Cancer cells have damaged mitochondria and can only use glucose (fermentation), not ketones. Healthy cells can switch between both (metabolic flexibility).

## Warburg Effect

Discovered by Otto Warburg (Nobel Prize, 1920s):
- Cancer cells rely almost entirely on glucose
- Even with oxygen present, they ferment glucose inefficiently
- They cannot metabolize ketones for energy

## Therapeutic Ketosis Strategy

**Goal**: Starve cancer while healthy cells thrive on ketones.

**Mechanism:**
1. Lower blood glucose (reduce carbs/sugar)
2. Raise ketone levels (high fat, moderate protein)
3. Cancer cells deprived of fuel; healthy cells switch to ketones

## Clinical Evidence

- Glioblastoma: Human trials show improved outcomes when keto added to standard treatment
- Case study (Fred): Terminal metastatic cancer → complete remission after ketogenic diet
- Multiple oncologists now include metabolic therapy

## Practical Implementation

**Eliminate:**
- Sugar
- Starch carbohydrates (bread, pasta, grains)

**Emphasize:**
- Healthy fats
- Moderate protein
- Low-carb vegetables

## Limitations

- Adjunct therapy, not standalone replacement for conventional treatment
- Works best for glucose-dependent tumors
- Requires medical supervision

## Why Suppressed

Cannot be patented → no pharmaceutical profit → limited mainstream adoption despite evidence.

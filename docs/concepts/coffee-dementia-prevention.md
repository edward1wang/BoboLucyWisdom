# Coffee and Dementia Prevention

**Harvard 43-year study (n=131,821):** Morning coffee reduces dementia risk by 18%. Decaf has **zero effect**.

## Mechanism: Adenosine Antagonism

- Adenosine builds up during day → causes sleepiness + suppresses memory neurotransmitters
- Caffeine blocks adenosine receptors **before** adenosine binds
- Memory circuits run less suppressed

## Why Decaf Fails

Decaf contains:
- Same polyphenols
- Same antioxidants  
- Same magnesium

**Missing:** The molecule that blocks adenosine receptors.

If polyphenols were the mechanism, decaf would show partial effect. It shows **zero**.

## Dose-Response

- Plateau around **3 cups**
- 5 cups ≠ better than 3 (just jitters)
- Effect holds even for high genetic risk

## Key Quote

> "Coffee is the most consumed psychoactive drug on the planet. 2 billion cups per day. We've spent decades treating it like a mild vice. Turns out it's the largest accidental dementia prevention program in human history."

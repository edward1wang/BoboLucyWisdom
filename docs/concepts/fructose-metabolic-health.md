# Fructose and Metabolic Health

**Nature Metabolism** review: Fructose isn't just sugar—it's an **evolutionary survival signal** that triggers fat storage, appetite increase, and metabolic slowdown.

## Evolutionary Context

- Fructose appears in autumn fruits = "winter coming" signal
- Triggers: stop fat burning, store energy, lower metabolism, increase appetite
- Modern problem: this signal is **on 365 days/year** via processed foods

## Metabolic Mechanism

| Glucose | Fructose |
|---------|----------|
| PFK1 enzyme regulates (brakes when ATP sufficient) | **No brakes!** (KHK-C enzyme) |
| Controlled energy | Uncontrolled ATP consumption |
| Satiety signal | "Pseudo-starvation" signal → eat more |

**Result:** Fat breakdown blocked while liver synthesizes new fat (DNL).

## Systemic Damage

- **Fatty liver** (metabolism similar to alcohol)
- **Brain:** Reduces blood flow to self-control + memory regions; linked to Alzheimer's
- **Blood pressure:** Through vasopressin stimulation
- **Cancer:** Fuels tumor growth

## Endogenous Fructose

Even without eating fructose, body can manufacture it via **polyol pathway**:
- High blood sugar
- Alcohol consumption
- Dehydration

## Practical Recommendations

**AVOID:**
- Fruit juice, bubble tea, sugary drinks
- High-fructose corn syrup
- "Healthy" sugars: agave (highest fructose!), excessive honey

**SMART CHOICES:**
- Low-sugar, high-fiber: blueberries, strawberries, raspberries, avocado, lemon
- Limit: lychee, grapes, ripe bananas, cantaloupe
- Timing: After meals, with nuts
- Amount: One fist-sized portion daily

**SUPPORT:**
- 2-3L water daily (prevents endogenous production)
- Limit alcohol
- Low-carb/keto diets reduce substrate
- Vitamin C + polyphenols combat uric acid damage

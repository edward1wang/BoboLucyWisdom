# Harvard Longitudinal Studies

The **Study of Adult Development** (1938-present) — world's longest-running longitudinal study of happiness and health.

## Study Design

- **Started:** 1938
- **Participants:** 724 men (two groups)
  - Group 1: Harvard students
  - Group 2: Boston impoverished youth
- **Duration:** 80+ years
- **Method:** Interviews every 2 years

## Key Finding

**#1 predictor of longevity at age 80:** Warm, close relationships at age 50

Quality > quantity. Loneliness as damaging as smoking.

## Leadership

- **George Vaillant** — Study director for 30+ years
- Compiled findings in *Triumphs of Experience*

## Impact

Contradicted assumptions about wealth, IQ, genetics as longevity predictors.

    2026 05 04 研究表明大约五分之一的CEO表现出具有临床意义的精神病态特征 2051195123573334376

    Post¶

    研究表明，大约五分之一的CEO表现出具有临床意义的精神病态特征，这一数字与监狱人口中观察到的情况非常相似。 澳大利亚心理学会年会上公布的研究结果揭示了高层领导中一个引人注目的模式：约21%的高级专业人士表现出这些特征。这一比例远高于普通人群中估计的1%至4%。 这些人常被描述为“成功的精神病态者”，他们凭借个人魅力、自信和极具说服力的性格，能够攀升至高层职位。然而，这些特质，如果再加上缺乏同理心和操控倾向，则会增加他们做出不道德决策的风险，并对组织造成长期损害。 据法医心理学家内森·布鲁克斯（Nathan Brooks）称，招聘方式可能在其中发挥了作用。布鲁克斯与邦德大学和圣地亚哥大学的同事共同领导了这项研究。许多公司优先考虑经验和技术能力，而忽视了有问题的性格特征。 该研究认为，在招聘过程中引入更严格的人格评估可以帮助组织避免那些为了短期利益而牺牲长期稳定和道德标准的领导者。 来源：Brooks, N.、Fritzon, K. 和 Croom, S.《企业精神病态：强调招聘过程中人格筛选的重要性》。澳大利亚心理学会年会。 Science girl (@sciencegirl) Research suggests that roughly one in five CEOs display clinically meaningful psychopathic traits, figures that closely resemble those observed within prison populations. Findings presented at the Australian Psychological Society annual congress highlight a striking pattern in senior leadership: around 21% of high-level professionals exhibit these traits. This is far higher than the estimated 1%–4% seen in the general population. Often described as “successful psychopaths,” such individuals can rise to top positions by relying on charm, confidence, and persuasive personalities. Yet those same characteristics, paired with low empathy and a tendency toward manipulation can increase the risk of unethical decision-making and long-term harm to organisations. According to forensic psychologist Nathan Brooks, who led the research with colleagues from Bond University and the University of San Diego, hiring practices may play a role. Many companies prioritise experience and technical ability while overlooking problematic personality traits. The study argues that introducing stronger personality assessments into recruitment could help organisations avoid leaders who achieve short-term gains at the expense of long-term stability and ethical standards. Source: Brooks, N., Fritzon, K., & Croom, S. Corporate Psychopathy: Highlighting the Importance of Personality Screening in the Recruitment Process.
